Notable Projects

Teensy 3.x

After years of using Teensy development boards, I wanted to dig into their inner workings. In 2019 I started writing firmware from scratch for the Teensy 3's K20DX256 microcontroller, and later teamed up with a programmer from France who had started working on some firmware of their own.

Our efforts involved reading much of the K20DX256's 1400 page reference manual, writing linker files, implementing registers and abstract device functionality, and creating examples and documentation. In the future we'd like to revisit the project with SVD-based code generation.

dorc - “Devin's Orchestrator”

dorc is a utility I made for simple green-blue deployments without the hassle of kubernetes or docker swarm. I use it for deploying my website with nearly zero downtime on my multi-purpose Linode VPS.

This project is split into two parts: the CLI, and the daemon. The daemon is a long-running service that watches a release directory, listens for commands via named pipes, and controls routing to a green or blue service. The CLI merely sets up SystemD Unit files when you register an application, and writes commands to the relevant named pipe.

Graphics Programming with WGPU

While I was experimenting with my custom game engine for a 2D game called void, I wanted to figure out a.) how to build my render graph, and b.) how to effectively render thousands of unique sprites.

In an attempt to resolve those questions, I made a particle rendering demo which draws and updates hundreds of thousands of individual particles every frame. Learning to make a renderer like this involved understanding low-level graphics APIs, writing compute, vertex, and fragment shaders in WGSL (and previously GLSL), and asking for plenty of help from niche online communities.
